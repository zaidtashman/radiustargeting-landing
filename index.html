Defining the Concept

Radius Targeting (often referred to as proximity targeting or geo-fencing) is a precision advertising strategy. It allows marketers to serve digital ads strictly to users located within a defined radial distance from a specific coordinate.

Unlike broad location targeting—which relies on arbitrary administrative lines like Cities or Zip Codes—radius targeting focuses purely on physical proximity.

How it works technically

  • The Center Point: You define a precise latitude and longitude coordinate.

  • The Perimeter: You set a distance (e.g., 1 mile). The ad platform draws a perfect circle around that point.

  • The Fence: GPS signals inside the circle trigger the ad. Signals outside are excluded.

This capability is available on Google Ads and Meta Ads, but manual implementation is often simplistic. The true power lies in micro-clustering.

The "Zip Code" Trap

Most marketers default to Zip Code targeting. However, Zip Codes are designed for mail delivery, not marketing efficiency. A single Zip Code often contains significant "dead zones":

  • Industrial Parks & Warehouses
  • Lakes, State Parks, and Farmland
  • Low-propensity neighborhoods

Targeting the whole region means paying for impressions in empty spaces.

The Solution: AI-Generated Pockets

Advanced Radius Targeting utilizes AI analysis to generate hundreds of micro-radii to cover specific demographic pockets.

  1. Input Demographics: Define your audience (e.g., "Household Income > $100k").
  2. AI Analysis: Algorithms scan geospatial data to find high-density clusters.
  3. Pocket Generation: The system places optimized circles only over those clusters.

Why humans can't do this manually: A human would need to cross-reference maps with census data and pin-drop 100+ locations per city. AI-driven tools automate this micro-segmentation instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Geo-fencing and Radius Targeting?

They are often used interchangeably. However, Geo-fencing can sometimes refer to custom polygon shapes, whereas Radius Targeting specifically refers to circular zones defined by a center point and a distance.

Does Radius Targeting lower CPA?

Yes. By excluding low-relevance geographic areas (like lakes or industrial zones), you reduce wasted impressions, which typically lowers your Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) and increases ROAS.
